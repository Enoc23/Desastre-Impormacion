Landslide

What is a Landslide?

A landslide is the movement of rock, soil, and debris down a slope due to gravity. It is a type of mass wasting, which denotes the down-slope movement of soil and rock under the influence of gravity.

How Do Landslides Happen?


Landslides can be triggered by several natural and human-induced factors:
1. Heavy Rainfall: Saturates the soil, reducing its cohesiveness and leading to slope failure.
2. Earthquakes: Shaking can loosen the soil and rock, causing it to slide.
3. Volcanic Activity: Volcanic eruptions can produce lahars and landslides.
4. Erosion: Gradual removal of slope material by water, wind, or ice weakens the slope.
5. Deforestation: Removal of trees and vegetation, which stabilizes the soil, can increase landslide risk.
6. Construction: Excavation and other construction activities can destabilize slopes.

The Strongest Landslide in the Philippines

One of the most devastating landslides in the Philippines occurred on February 17, 2006, in the village of Guinsaugon, Southern Leyte. Following a period of heavy rainfall, a massive landslide buried the entire village, resulting in over a thousand deaths. The disaster was exacerbated by deforestation in the area.

Areas Affected by Landslides in the Philippines


Landslide-prone areas in the Philippines include:
- Cordillera Administrative Region: Mountainous terrain prone to landslides, especially during typhoons and heavy rains.
- Eastern Visayas: Particularly Southern Leyte, where steep slopes and heavy rainfall are common.
- Bicol Region: Known for volcanic activity and heavy rains that can trigger landslides.
- Mindanao: Areas like Compostela Valley and Davao Oriental, prone to landslides due to mining and deforestation activities.

Types of Landslides: Causes, Characteristics, and Affected Areas


1. Falls
- Causes: Weathering, earthquakes, and erosion.
- Characteristics: Free fall of rock or debris from steep slopes or cliffs.
- Affected Areas: Steep cliffs and mountainous regions.
2. Slides
- Causes Heavy rainfall, earthquakes, and human activities like mining and construction.
- Characteristics: Movement of a mass of rock, earth, or debris along a defined surface.
- Affected Areas: Sloped terrains with loose soil and rock.
3. Flows
- Causes: Saturation of material with water, leading to flow-like movement.
- Characteristics: Fluid-like motion of saturated soil and debris, often occurring rapidly.
- Affected Areas: Areas with loose, water-saturated soils, such as riverbanks and coastal regions.
4. Creeps
- Causes: Slow, continuous movement due to freeze-thaw cycles, water saturation, and gravity.
- Characteristics: Gradual down-slope movement of soil and rock.
- Affected Areas: Gentle slopes and areas with frequent freeze-thaw cycles.

Tips and Guidelines to Prevent and Prepare for Landslides


Prevention and Preparedness
- Slope Management: Implement proper land-use planning and slope management practices to reduce the risk.
- Vegetation: Plant trees and vegetation to stabilize slopes.
- Drainage Control: Ensure proper drainage systems to prevent water accumulation on slopes.
- Retaining Structures: Build retaining walls and terraces to support unstable slopes.
- Community Education: Raise awareness and educate communities on landslide risks and preparedness measures.

Safety Measures to Follow


Before a Landslide

- Stay Informed: Monitor weather reports and landslide warnings. - Assess Risk: Identify landslide-prone areas in your vicinity. - Emergency Plan: Develop an emergency plan and evacuation routes for your family. - Reinforce Property: Use proper engineering practices to reinforce your property against landslides.

During a Landslide

- Evacuate Immediately: If you suspect an imminent landslide, move to higher ground quickly. - Stay Alert: Watch for signs of landslides, such as cracks in the ground, unusual sounds (like rumbling or trees cracking), or sudden water flow changes. - Shelter in Place: If evacuation is not possible, shelter in the most secure part of your home, such as an interior room on the upper floor.

After a Landslide

- Stay Away: Avoid landslide-affected areas until authorities declare them safe. - Inspect for Hazards: Be cautious of potential hazards such as damaged roads, bridges, and downed power lines. - Assist Others: Help those in need and cooperate with rescue and relief operations. - Report Damages: Document and report any structural damage to local authorities for assessment and assistance.
