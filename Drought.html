Drought

What is Drought?

A drought is an extended period of deficient rainfall relative to the statistical multi-year average for a region. It results in water shortages that affect the environment, agriculture, human health, and the economy.

How Does Drought Happen?

Droughts occur due to a combination of natural and human factors:
Meteorological Factors: Prolonged periods of low precipitation, which can be influenced by atmospheric conditions like high-pressure systems that prevent cloud formation.
Hydrological Factors: Reduced water availability in rivers, reservoirs, and groundwater supplies due to lack of rainfall and increased evaporation rates.
-Agricultural Factors: Soil moisture deficits that impact crop growth and water availability for livestock.
- Human Factors: Overuse and mismanagement of water resources, deforestation, and climate change exacerbate the natural occurrences of drought.

Areas Affected by Drought in the Philippines


Drought in the Philippines typically affects:
- Luzon: Particularly Central Luzon and Metro Manila, due to high population density and water demand.
- Visayas: Including Eastern Visayas and parts of Western and Central Visayas.
- Mindanao: Particularly in agricultural areas like North Cotabato and Davao del Sur.

Types of Drought, Causes, Characteristics, and Affected Areas


1. Meteorological Drought:
- Causes: Below-average precipitation.
- Characteristics: Reduced rainfall, increased temperature, and altered wind patterns.
- Affected Areas: Can occur anywhere, but regions like Central Luzon and Mindanao are particularly vulnerable.
2. Agricultural Drought:
- Causes: Insufficient soil moisture to meet the needs of crops.
- Characteristics: Poor crop yield, reduced soil fertility, and stunted plant growth.
- Affected Areas: Predominantly agricultural regions such as Central Luzon, Western Visayas, and Mindanao.
3. Hydrological Drought:
- Causes: Decreased water levels in lakes, rivers, and reservoirs.
- Characteristics: Lowered water supply for domestic, agricultural, and industrial use.
- Affected Areas: Areas dependent on surface water and groundwater, including urban centers like Metro Manila.
4. Socioeconomic Drought:
- Causes: A combination of the above factors leading to a supply-demand mismatch.
- Characteristics: Economic losses, food and water scarcity, and public health issues.
- Affected Areas: Broad regions where agricultural and economic activities are heavily impacted, such as Luzon and Mindanao.

Tips and Guidelines to Prevent and Prepare for Drought

Prevention:


- Water Conservation: Implement water-saving techniques in households, industries, and agriculture.
- Sustainable Practices: Adopt sustainable farming practices like crop rotation and the use of drought-resistant crops.
- Afforestation: Plant trees to enhance the water cycle and reduce evaporation.

Preparation:


- Water Storage: Build and maintain reservoirs and rainwater harvesting systems.
- Drought Planning: Develop drought contingency plans at local and national levels.
- Early Warning Systems: Use meteorological data to predict and monitor drought conditions.

Safety Measures Before, During, and After a Drought


Before a Drought:


- Educate the Community: Raise awareness about water conservation and drought preparedness.
- Infrastructure: Improve water infrastructure to reduce leaks and waste.
- Emergency Plans: Establish clear protocols for water rationing and relief distribution.

During a Drought:


- Water Restrictions: Enforce water usage restrictions for non-essential activities.
- Support Farmers: Provide assistance to farmers through subsidies, alternative crops, and efficient irrigation methods.
- Health Measures: Ensure adequate hydration and sanitation to prevent health issues.

After a Drought:


- Recovery Programs: Implement programs to restore affected ecosystems and support economic recovery.
- Water Management Review: Assess and improve water management practices to better prepare for future droughts.
- Community Support: Offer psychological and financial support to those severely affected by the drought.
