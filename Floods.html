Floods

What are Floods?

Floods are overflow of water that submerges land which is usually dry. They are among the most common and destructive natural disasters, causing significant damage to property and loss of life.

How Do Floods Happen?


Floods can occur due to a variety of reasons, including:
1. Heavy Rainfall: Prolonged or intense rainstorms can overwhelm the capacity of rivers, streams, and drainage systems.
2. River Overflow: When rivers exceed their capacity due to excessive rainfall or snowmelt.
3. Storm Surges: High winds from tropical storms and hurricanes push seawater onto the shore.
4. Dam Failure: Structural failure of dams or levees can release large volumes of water.
5. Urban Runoff: In cities, paved surfaces can prevent water from being absorbed, leading to flash floods.
6. Melting Snow and Ice: Rapid melting of snow or ice can cause rivers and streams to overflow.

The Strongest Floods in the Philippines

One of the most catastrophic floods in the Philippines was Typhoon Ondoy (Ketsana) in September 2009. The typhoon brought an unprecedented amount of rainfall, equivalent to a month's worth of rain in just six hours, causing severe flooding in Metro Manila and nearby provinces. The floods resulted in hundreds of deaths and extensive damage to property and infrastructure.

Areas Affected by Floods in the Philippines


Flood-prone areas in the Philippines include:
- Metro Manila: Due to heavy urbanization and poor drainage systems.
- Central Luzon: Including Pampanga and Bulacan, often affected by river overflows.
- Cagayan Valley: Due to river flooding from Cagayan River.
- Eastern Visayas: Especially Leyte and Samar, frequently hit by typhoons and heavy rains.
- Bicol Region: Vulnerable to typhoons and heavy rainfall.
- Mindanao: Areas like Cagayan de Oro and Iligan are prone to flash floods.

Types of Floods: Causes, Characteristics, and Affected Areas


1. River Floods
- Causes: Heavy rainfall, snowmelt, or dam failure.
- Characteristics: Gradual rise in water levels, affecting riverbanks and surrounding areas.
- Affected Areas: Floodplains and low-lying areas near rivers.
2. Flash Floods
- Causes: Intense, short-duration rainfall or sudden release of water from dams.
- Characteristics: Rapid onset, often with little warning, causing swift and dangerous water flows.
- Affected Areas: Urban areas with poor drainage, steep terrains, and valleys.
3. Coastal Floods
- Causes: Storm surges, high tides, and tsunamis.
- Characteristics: Inundation of coastal areas by seawater.
- Affected Areas: Coastal regions and low-lying islands.
4. Urban Floods
- Causes: Heavy rainfall, inadequate drainage systems, and high levels of impermeable surfaces.
- Characteristics: Water accumulation in urban areas, often causing significant disruption.
- Affected Areas: Cities and towns.
5. Pluvial Floods
- Causes: Intense rainfall leading to surface water accumulation.
- Characteristics: Occurs when drainage systems are overwhelmed, not necessarily near rivers or coasts.
- Affected Areas: Anywhere with heavy rainfall and poor drainage.

Tips and Guidelines to Prevent and Prepare for Floods

Prevention and Preparedness


- Early Warning Systems: Install and maintain flood detection and warning systems.
- Community Education: Educate the public on flood risks and safety measures.
- Infrastructure Planning: Design and maintain effective drainage systems and flood barriers.
- Land Use Management: Avoid building in flood-prone areas and protect natural flood buffers like wetlands.
- Emergency Kits: Prepare kits with essentials such as food, water, medications, and important documents.

Safety Measures to Follow

Before a Flood


- Stay Informed: Monitor weather forecasts and flood warnings.
- Prepare an Emergency Plan: Identify evacuation routes and safe shelters.
- Secure Property: Elevate electrical appliances, seal important documents in waterproof containers, and build barriers to prevent water entry.
- Evacuation Readiness: Be ready to evacuate immediately if advised by authorities.

During a Flood


- Evacuate Immediately: Follow evacuation orders and move to higher ground.
- Avoid Floodwaters: Do not walk, swim, or drive through floodwaters as they may be deeper and faster-moving than they appear.
- Stay Informed: Listen to weather updates and instructions from authorities.

After a Flood


- Wait for the All Clear: Do not return home until authorities declare it safe.
- Inspect for Hazards: Check for structural damage, gas leaks, and electrical hazards before entering buildings.
- Avoid Contaminated Water: Do not use tap water until it is declared safe, and be cautious of waterborne diseases.
- Document Damage: Take photos and keep records for insurance claims and assistance.
